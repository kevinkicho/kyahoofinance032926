Color logic: GDP% — green if >2%, red if <0%. CPI% — green if 1–3%, red if >5%. Policy Rate — contextual (high = red in low-growth env). Unemp — green if <5%, red if >8%. Debt/GDP — green if <60%, red if >100%.

Sub-view 2: Growth & Inflation

Two ECharts panels side-by-side. Left: GDP growth YoY% bar chart (latest year, all 12 countries ranked). Right: CPI inflation YoY% — same ranking. Teal bars for positive growth, red for negative/high inflation. Lets you instantly see growth vs inflation tradeoffs across the world.

Sub-view 3: Central Bank Rates

Two panels. Top: Current policy rates ranked as a horizontal bar chart (highest to lowest) — immediately shows the global rate dispersion. Bottom: 5-year history of rates for G7 central banks as a multi-line ECharts chart — shows the synchronized hike cycle and divergence. Reference line at 2% (neutral rate).

Sub-view 4: Debt Monitor

Two panels. Left: Government debt as % of GDP — horizontal bar chart, all 12 countries, color-coded (green <60%, amber 60–90%, red >90% per Maastricht criteria). Right: Current account balance as % of GDP — bars showing surplus/deficit by country. Surplus countries (green, above zero) vs deficit countries (red, below). Tells the capital flow story.

Data Sources

World Bank API (free, no key) — GDP growth, CPI, unemployment, debt/GDP, current account for all 12 countries. Annual data.
FRED (existing key) — Monthly policy rates for US, ECB, UK, Japan, Canada, Australia, Sweden (7/12).
Mock — Policy rates for China, India, Brazil, South Korea, Mexico (updated in mock data).

Server endpoint: /api/globalMacro · Cache: 3600s (World Bank data is annual; daily refresh is fine).
