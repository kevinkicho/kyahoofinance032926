Global Macro — Country Scope

How broad should the country coverage be?

A

G7 Core (7 economies)

US · Euro Area · UK · Japan · Canada · France · Italy

The traditional advanced-economy bloc. Cleanest data coverage via FRED. All 7 have complete, consistent historical series going back decades. Focused and comparable — ideal for yield curve / rate cycle analysis.

Pros

  • Full FRED coverage
  • Decades of history
  • Clean comparisons

Cons

  • Misses China, India, EM
B

G7 + Key EM (12 economies) — Recommended

US · Euro Area · UK · Japan · Canada · China · India · Brazil · South Korea · Australia · Mexico · Sweden

Adds the four largest emerging markets plus Australia and South Korea — the economies that drive global commodity demand and supply chains. World Bank API fills in EM data gaps. A richer picture of global growth dynamics.

Pros

  • China/India growth story
  • Commodity demand context
  • True global picture

Cons

  • EM data lags by 1 quarter
  • More complex layout
C

Full G20 (20 economies)

All G20 members including Saudi Arabia, Argentina, South Africa, Turkey, Indonesia, Russia

Maximum coverage. Some members have spotty data and significant political/methodology risk. Useful for a "world map" view but harder to compare cleanly. Best for the Debt Monitor sub-view, not ideal for policy rate comparisons.

Pros

  • Complete global coverage
  • World map visualization

Cons

  • Inconsistent data quality
  • Some series are sparse

My recommendation: B (G7 + Key EM, 12 economies) — broad enough to tell the global growth story, narrow enough to keep data clean and comparisons meaningful.
